Severity Classification

Not all losses are equal. The 12 losses fall into four categories.

Negligible (5 losses)
RT sRGB, RT P3, RT Rec.2020, Gray ramp C*, 1000-trip RT — All at 10-7 to 10-15 scale. Both spaces are at machine precision; differences are meaningless in practice.
Genuine Trade-offs (5 losses)
Gradient CV p95, Primary L range, Blue G/R midpoint, CVD protan, CVD deutan — Real weaknesses tied to the enriched pipeline design. The deuteranopia separation gap (-69%) is the most significant.
Self-referential (1 loss)
Munsell V: 0.00 vs 2.80 — Munsell data was computed in CIE Lab / Y1/3 coordinates. Any space not sharing that basis will score non-zero. This is a bias in the benchmark, not a weakness of v14.
Structural (1 loss)
Hue agreement CIE Lab: 32.6° vs 8.5° — v14 deliberately rotates hue angles away from CIE Lab conventions for better perceptual uniformity. Higher disagreement with CIE Lab is expected behavior.
